ENGL 4804: Grant Proposals and Reports

Course Description

This course prepares students to write effective proposals, reports, and informational articles. Students learn to define and write problem statements, program objectives, plans of action, evaluation plans, budget presentations, and summaries. In addition, they sharpen their teamwork, editing, writing, audience awareness, and design skills as they engage in collaborative projects with campus and/or non-profit organizations in the community.

Professor

James Dubinsky

Professor's Background

James Dubinsky is an associate professor of English at Virginia Tech. He is the founding director of the Department of English's Professional Writing Program and helped shape the first liberal arts PhD at Virginia Tech in Rhetoric and Writing. He is the founding director of the Center for Student Engagement and Community Partnerships (now VT-Engage) and the lead faculty member in the Veterans in Society Initiative. His work focuses on the scholarship of teaching, service learning, professional and technical communication, and veterans in society.

He received his BA in English from Wake Forest University in Winston-Salem, NC; MA in English from University of California at Berkeley in Berkeley, CA; and PhD in English (focusing on Rhetoric, Composition, and Professional Communication) from Miami University (Ohio) in Oxford, OH.

At Virginia Tech, he has taught many undergraduate and graduate courses including:

  • ENGL 3104: Professional Writing
  • ENGL 4804: Grant Proposals and Reports
  • ENGL 5654: Theory and Practice of Teaching Professional Writing

He received the National Endowment for the Humanities Summer Institute Grant with Dr. Bruce Pencek in July 2015 for $152,000 to host a summer institute on American Veterans in Society for university teachers at Virginia Tech. He has published articles such as “Product Documentation to Integrated Technical Content.” in Technical Communication in 2015. He also published a book titled Teaching Technical Writing: Critical Issues for the Classroom in 2004.

My Experience in the Course

In this course, I am learning how to effectively write grant proposals. While we have not started any writing yet, we are focusing on what is arguably the most important: the why. Establishing who you are and your mission and then finding a grant that aligns with your goals is just as important as the actual writing. Our final project is to research a grant and prepare a proposal for that grant. We are now researching grants and organizations to decide what to apply for.
