ENGL 4814: Developing Online Content

Course Description

Covers the process of creating documents for online environments. Builds on knowledge and skills acquired in foundational Professional Writing courses. Involves production of websites from scratch, starting with low-fidelity mockups and advancing to formatting layouts adaptable to the diverse screen sizes of computers and mobile devices. Focuses on a balance of structure (code), content (information), and format (presentation and design).

Professor

Carlos Evia

Professor's Background

Carlos Evia is the Associate Dean for Transdisciplinary Initiatives, Chief Technology Officer, and a professor in the School of Communication located in the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences at Virginia Tech. His research focuses include technical and professional communication, user assistance, and content strategy.

He received his LCC in from the Instituto de Ciencias Sociales de Merida in Yucatan, Mexico; MSC from the Universidad LaSelle in Mexico City, Mexico; and PhD from Texas Tech University in Lubbock, Texas.

Recent journal articles include “Preparing the Next Generation of Leaders and Innovators in Technical Communication.” published in Intercom: The Magazine of the Society for Technical Communication in 2018 (coauthored with Rebekka Andersen) and “Structured authoring without XML: Evaluating Lightweight DITA for technical documentation.” in Technical Communication in 2016 (coauthored with Michael Priestley). He has published a book titled Creating Intelligent Content with Lightweight DITA in 2018.

My Experience in the Course

In this course, I am learning how to create digital content from structuring a website’s code using HTML to writing the website’s content to finally designing the website itself. We learned the basics of HTML and how to use AI to help us structure our website. I have skills from previous courses that help me to write content, but creating a website is totally new to me. I am enjoying learning these new skills and our future projects will build on that as we create more comprehensive websites.
