ENGL 4874: Issues in Professional and Public Discourse

Course Description

In this course designed for English majors in the Professional Writing Option, students will focus on the ways in which scientific, technical, and professional communication influence, and are influenced by, public discourse. Drawing on strategies of rhetorical criticism, students will gain an understanding of the persuasive value of style, arrangement, and delivery by investigating their professional roles in helping to structure public debate.

Professor

Sweta Baniya

Professor's Background

Sweta Baniya is an assistant professor of rhetoric, professional and technical writing at Virginia Tech. She specializes in transnational disaster studies, risk/crisis communication, non-Western rhetorical traditions, and community engagement. Her works have appeared in a variety of journals including Encultration, Journal of Business & Technical Communication, and Journal of Technological Studies. She is also a founding member of nextGEN, an international graduate student listserv.

She received her Master of Arts in English from Pokhara University in Kathmandu, Nepal; Master of Philosophy in English from Tribhuvan University in Kathmandu, Nepal; and PhD in English (Rhetoric and Composition) from Purdue University in Lafayette, IN.

At Virginia Tech, she has taught many English courses including:

  • ENGL 3104: Professional Writing
  • ENGL 3814: Creating User Documentation
  • ENGL 4874: Issues in Professional and Public Discourse

Looking at her featured works, she most recently released her book Transnational Assemblages: Social Justice and Crisis Communication during Disaster in July 2024. Recent journal articles include “Transnational Assemblages in Disaster Response: Networked Communities, Technologies, and Coalitional Actions During Global Disasters” published in Technical Communication Quarterly in 2022 and “How Can Technical Communicators Help in Disaster Response?” published in Technical Communications in 2022.

My Experience in the Course

In this course, I have learned what crisis communication is and the importance of it. Any time there is a crisis, whether a physical or technological disaster, it affects an entire community. Communication is one of the most critical things people need during that time. Without communication, there is chaos and misunderstanding. We have analyzed disasters such as the Exxon Valdez oil spill in which the communication response was not effective versus the British Petroleum oil spill in which communication was prompt, reassuring, and efficient. Our final project for this course is a conference paper relating to a topic within disaster studies/crisis communication. We are now researching and deciding on topics.
